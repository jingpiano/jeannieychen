1. Jinling High School (Nanjing) 南京金陵中学

On September 2, 2019, Jinling High School/UCLA Global Classroom officially began the Fall 2019 term. This is a view from the 31st floor of my apartment complex of the beautiful high school campus. From the talented students I work with as a teaching aide during the day and all the wonderful people I have met, to walking the busy, historic, colorful streets of Xinjiekou (新街口) in downtown Nanjing, there is no end to the surprises and new experiences I've had here!

Read more about the UCLA Global Classroom Program in Nanjing.


2. Nanjing Library 南京图书馆

With more than 9.1 million volumes total, the Nanjing Library (南京图书馆) is the Jiangsu provincial public library and a first-class, historic library named by the Culture Ministry. This library was established in 1907, located in the central area of Daxinggong (大行宫) and accessible to many nearby services, shopping places, restaurants, and residential areas. Its motto is: "To treasure time, to advocate thought, to promote open intelligence, and to show aboveboard virtue." See this broad overview of the Nanjing Library for more information on its services, collections, and preservation endeavors in relation to the rich Chinese history and culture.


3. The City of Nanjing

The city of Nanjing has a special place in my family's history. My grandmother, who raised me from my birth in the hospital all the way to my high school graduation in the U.S., returned to Nanjing to live during the rest of her retirement. The area surrounding her home has become my haven to which I have returned multiple times, to see my grandmother and to revisit a place that holds special memories and reveals new wonders for me each time I go there. Enjoying this uniquely green campus, my grandmother would take her daily 6:00 A.M. and 4:00 P.M. walks through forests planted in front of her complex, meander down traditional Chinese garden paths, and stroll past artificial lakes with floating bridges, walkways, and lotus flower beds.


4. A Sea of Marigolds 万寿菊之海

Did you know? Marigolds are also known in Chinese as the "Ten Thousand-Year Flower" (万寿菊). My grandmother's full-time nanny/caretaker was showing me photographs she took of a beautiful array of marigolds. I thought it would be special to take some photos of my grandmother and I together, and send them to my family, many of whom live in the U.S. With a pristine blue sky and the beautiful "Ten Thousand-Year Flower" in full bloom, my grandmother and I spent a peaceful morning enjoying this sea of colour before heading back home for breakfast.


5. Sunlit Lake 阳光照射的湖

One of my favorite areas of where my grandmother lives is the artificial lakes with its winding bridges, encircled by trees and people dancing and doing tai-chi (太极拳) in the coolness of the early morning. Even though at the end of September, standing bodies of water are usually magnets for mosquitoes (and I had my share of painful bites and allergic reactions), it's worth it for every second I can spare to spend with my grandmother. I chose this afternoon scene for its blue sky, white clouds, and greenery reflected in the calming lake waters.
