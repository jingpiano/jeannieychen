Licences & Certificates

TEFL in China | State Administration of Foreign Experts Affairs (SAFEA)

  • TEFL in China was launched as a teacher training program in 2006, by the State Administration of Foreign Expert Affairs (SAFEA). It is an accelerated program, with the most necessary materials and discussion for teachers of EFL in China. Hundreds of trainees have received TEFL in China certification, which is recognized both inside China and internationally.
  • The core training program of TEFL in China is designed for general English language instruction. Materials are designed to be applied to a wide range of students, young or mature, novice or advanced. The aim is to equip teachers with an array of materials and a repertoire of techniques that will work in almost any classroom.
  • In addition to teaching resources, TEFL in China is designed to help foreign teachers become aware of many of the subtleties of life in China, in hopes that their work in Chinese institutions will be well received.

Certificate of Completion | American Library Association (ALA)

  • This six-week eCourse provides a detailed introduction to the core concepts, functions, and tools of music collection development. The course instructors are Houman Behzadi, Lisa Hooper, and John Redford.
  • Over the course of pre-recorded video lessons, readings, and assignments, learneres develop their own collecting philosophy and gain a deeper understanding of collection development policies, ownership vs access, collecting strategies, budget management, format-based selection tools, firm orders, standing orders, and approval plans.
  • Instructors will note the commonalities and differences in how core concepts are adapted to local institutional needs in small, medium, and large institutions.

Study Abroad

English 797J, Graphic Narratives of Britain and Its Former Colonies (Dr. Monica Chiu) | Gonville & Caius College

  • Monica Chiu is Professor of English at the University of New Hampshire, where she teaches Asian American studies, comics and graphic narratives, and American Studies. She is the editor of the collection Drawing New Color Lines: Transnational Asian American Graphic Narratives (Hong Kong UP) and author of the recently published Scrutinized! Surveillance in Asian North American Literature (U of Hawaii P, 2014).
  • This course introduces students to the medium of graphic narratives/comics through contemporary texts related to Britain and its former colonies. Through participatory lecture and discussion, this course addresses many comics terms and formal properties; visual culture; popular culture; representations of race & gender; manga.
  • Questions that drive daily discussions relate both to the medium and to political, historical, and cultural issues addressed in the graphic narrative include: How do you analyze comics? How is analyzing comics as a medium different from analyzing literature or film? What issues benefit the most/least from comics’ unique format? How do colonialism and post-colonialism play out in graphic narratives? How are comics read transnationally? How is race or ethnicity imagined/imaged?

English 797H, British Modernist Fiction: 'Difficult Music' (Dr. Rod Mengham) | Gonville & Caius College

  • Rod Mengham is Reader in Modern English Literature at the University of Cambridge, where he is also Curator of Works of Art at Jesus College. He is the author of books on Charles Dickens, Emily Brontë and Henry Green, as well as of The Descent of Language (1993). He was co-editor/co-translator of Altered State: the New Polish Poetry (Arc, 2003), and co-editor of Vanishing Points: New Modernist Poems (Salt Publishing, 2005).
  • 'It was like difficult music heard for the first time.'​ – Samuel Beckett
  • 'Beauty of music you must hear twice.' – James Joyce
  • The purpose of this course is to work closely with the texts of some key modern authors and to explore the ways in which the formal innovations of their writing may be related to developments in cultural history. Arguments about history, sexuality, language, politics and economics may be traced, in varying proportions, in both the matter and the manner of construction of all the specified texts, which have been chosen in order to demonstrate a characteristic variety of artistic and political outlooks.
