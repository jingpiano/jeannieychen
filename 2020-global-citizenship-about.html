About the Program

  What will participants learn?

  • Increase awareness of community & civic issues at the local and global level;
  • Prepare for the changing information landscape that requires a blend of research and media literacy skills;
  • Earn valuable leadership, debate, critical thinking, and presentation skills; and
  • Meet a group of highly motivated peers from different backgrounds.

  Yi Ding | CSU Northridge Faculty Librarian

  • Online Instruction Coordinator at California State University Northridge Library.
  • Former Inquiry Specialist at University of California at Los Angeles (UCLA) Library.
  • 2020 American Library Association (ALA) Emerging Leader.

  Jeannie Chen | UCLA Global Classroom Teacher (2019-2020)

  • 2019-2020 Teaching Aide at UCLA Global Classroom Program at Jinling High School in Nanjing, China.
  • 2017 Association of Research Libraries Kaleidoscope Program Scholar.
  • 2016 American Library Association (ALA) Spectrum Scholarship Awardee.

  All U.S. high school and freshman/sophomore college students who are:

  • able to access a device (computer, laptop, tablet) with Wi-Fi/Ethernet connection capabilities;
  • interested in engaging deeply with current issues and making an impact!

  Instruction will occur primarily using:
  • Zoom video conferencing platform; and
  • Canvas learning management system.

  This program structure addresses the following standards:

  • International Society for Technology in Education (ISTE) Standards for Students;
  • Association of College & Research Libraries (ACRL) Framework for Information Literacy; and
  • Common Core State Standards.
